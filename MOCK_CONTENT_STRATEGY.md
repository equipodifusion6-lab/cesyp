# MOCK_CONTENT_STRATEGY.md

# CESyP — Mock Institutional Content System

## Strategic Content Architecture for MVP

# OBJECTIVE

Generate a complete mock institutional ecosystem for CESyP that:

* feels real,
* feels active,
* feels intellectually serious,
* creates perceived institutional legitimacy,
* demonstrates digital potential,
* improves user retention,
* increases perceived organizational scale,
* communicates territorial and analytical presence.

The content must:

* be readable,
* editorial,
* visually engaging,
* politically neutral in tone,
* institutionally professional,
* informative without becoming bureaucratic.

The goal is NOT to simulate propaganda.

The goal is to simulate:

* a modern think tank,
* a territorial observatory,
* a policy and social analysis institution,
* an active civic organization.

---

# CORE CONTENT PHILOSOPHY

Every section of the platform should answer at least one of these questions:

1. What is happening in Jujuy?
2. Why does it matter?
3. Who is being affected?
4. What evidence exists?
5. What discussions are active?
6. What initiatives are underway?
7. What can citizens understand from this?
8. What is CESyP analyzing or documenting?

The platform should feel:

* alive,
* constantly updated,
* territorial,
* analytical,
* useful,
* dynamic.

---

# MAIN CONTENT STRUCTURE

The platform content must be divided into:

1. Institutional Identity
2. Editorial Articles
3. Territorial Reports
4. Social & Political Analysis
5. Events & Activities
6. Multimedia
7. Data & Indicators
8. Community Participation
9. Documents & Publications
10. Strategic Newsletters

* calm,
* credible,
* analytical,
* thoughtful.
